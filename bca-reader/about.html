More accurate than Excel

Not a dig at Excel. The problem is one specific habit: reading the trendline equation off the chart. Excel prints it rounded for display. On the plate this was built against it printed:

y = 0.0007x

The real slope was 0.000741. Type the label into a cell and every concentration comes out 5.9% high. You then pipette less sample than you meant to, so every lane loads about 5% under target. Nothing ever looks wrong, because every lane is wrong by the same amount.

This tool fits the curve to your ladder, every run, at full precision. No slope is ever typed into it, and nothing in it reads a number off a label. That is the whole of the advantage.

If you would rather just fix your spreadsheet, do that instead. It is one cell, and you do not need this tool for it:

=SLOPE(known_ys, known_xs)

No template to fiddle with

No workbook to copy, no cells to re-point when your ladder moves a row, no formula to break when someone inserts a column, and no hunting for whichever version of the sheet is the good one.

Paste your plate reader's readings, tag which wells are blanks, ladder, and samples, and BCA Reader gives you concentrations plus the µL of sample, lysis buffer, and dye per tube for equal protein mass in every lane. No plate reader? You can photograph the plate with your phone instead, at the cost of some accuracy.

How it was built

By a summer student at Baylor College of Medicine in 2026, against a real plate on a real plate reader rather than simulated data. The fit lands within about 1% of corrected truth, which is also how the rounding got caught: the tool and the spreadsheet disagreed by 6%, and the spreadsheet turned out to be wrong.

No experimental results from that lab appear anywhere in it.

What it is not good at

Phone photos. If you photograph the plate with your phone instead of reading it on a plate reader, the numbers get worse: they run about 5% high, and uneven lighting across the plate can push a single well past 10%. A camera is guessing at colour under whatever light your bench happens to have; a plate reader measures it.

So everything above about accuracy is the plate reader path, not the camera. If you have a plate reader, paste its readings. The phone photo is there for when you genuinely do not have the instrument. It is better than nothing, not equal to one.

Your data never leaves your computer

No server, no upload, no account, no analytics. It runs inside your browser tab and keeps working with the wifi off. It is free, and the code is on GitHub.

It is an educational tool built by a student, not a validated clinical instrument. Check its numbers before you trust a gel to them.
